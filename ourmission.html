Situation in India

India loses natural resources to economic growth

India is the second most populous country in the world, with more than 1 billion citizens. A staggering 344 million practice open defecation. The World Bank estimates that 21 percent of communicable diseases in India are linked to unsafe water and the lack of hygiene practices. Further, more than 500 children under the age of five die each day from diarrhea in India alone. In the last 6 years, the rate of growth of forest stock has reduced by more than 10% in almost all states.Climate change has had a huge impact on water resources.

How do we help ?

Solution

Our solution is to provide them jobs as bottle makers for our company which will help them earn more money to build wells for catching water from which they can make drinking water as well. Because this this approach need more supports, we made a deal with our partners that 15 percent of monthly revenue is going to be send to these villages for making more wells.
